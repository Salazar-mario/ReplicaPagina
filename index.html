About Dr. Norman Borlaug

Early Life

Dr. Norman Borlaug was born on March 25, 1914, in Cresco, Iowa. He grew up on a farm and developed a keen interest in agriculture and plant sciences at a young age.

Contributions

Dr. Borlaug's greatest achievement was his development of a high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat variety called "dwarf wheat." This variety revolutionized agriculture and saved countless lives.

Legacy

Dr. Norman Borlaug's work has had a lasting impact on global food production and has been recognized with numerous awards and honors.
